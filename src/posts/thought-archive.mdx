Over the last two years, I've used ChatGPT and Claude as thinking partners.

Not just for quick answers, but for real work: planning projects, working through decisions, and capturing ideas I didn’t want to lose.

Over time, that produced something surprisingly valuable: a huge archive of conversations containing ideas, lessons learned, half-finished projects, and moments of genuine insight.

The problem was that none of it was easy to find.

I often knew I had already thought through a problem before, but I couldn't remember where. So I'd end up asking the same questions again, rebuilding context from scratch, and spending time rediscovering things I'd already figured out months earlier.

This project started as an attempt to break that cycle.

I'm building a personal memory and search system that can look through my past conversations and surface the pieces most relevant to what I'm thinking about today.

---

## What I've Built So Far

So far, I've collected and processed roughly two years of chat history from ChatGPT and Claude.

The conversations are converted into a common format, split into smaller searchable chunks, and indexed so they can be searched together regardless of where they originally came from.

When I ask a question, the system doesn't just look for exact keywords. Instead, it searches for conversations that are semantically related to the topic I'm asking about.

In practical terms, that means I can ask questions like:

* "What was I most stressed about last year?"
* "What ideas have I had for side projects?"
* "What conclusions did I reach about X problem?"
* "What have I learned about Y topic?"

and retrieve relevant discussions from months or even years ago.

This is essentially Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) applied to my own history: retrieve relevant context first, then use that context to generate a more informed answer.

---

## What's Coming Next

Right now, the focus is less on adding more data and more on making the results genuinely useful.

I'm working on better filtering by time, topic, and source so I can ask more targeted questions and get more precise answers.

I'm also improving how retrieved conversations are presented. Instead of digging through walls of text, I want to be able to quickly scan the evidence, understand why it was selected, and spot patterns across multiple conversations.

Another priority is trustworthy summarization.

I don't want generic AI-generated reflections. I want summaries that are clearly grounded in things I actually said, thought, or discussed, with enough context that I can verify where the conclusions came from.

Longer term, the goal is a simple personal search experience:

- Ask a question.

- See the most relevant parts of my past thinking.

- Leave with something useful in a few minutes.
